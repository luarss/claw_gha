# Financial Terminology Reference

Quick reference for financial terms and ratios used in stock analysis.

---

## Valuation Terms

### P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
**Definition**: Stock price divided by earnings per share
**Use**: Measures how much investors pay for each dollar of earnings
**Formula**: `P/E = Stock Price / EPS`

### PEG Ratio
**Definition**: P/E ratio divided by earnings growth rate
**Use**: Adjusts P/E for growth rate; Lynch's favorite metric
**Formula**: `PEG = P/E / EPS Growth Rate`

### P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)
**Definition**: Stock price divided by book value per share
**Use**: Compares market value to accounting value of assets
**Formula**: `P/B = Stock Price / (Assets - Liabilities) per share`

### P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales)
**Definition**: Market cap divided by annual revenue
**Use**: Values companies without profits
**Formula**: `P/S = Market Cap / Revenue`

### EV (Enterprise Value)
**Definition**: Market cap plus debt minus cash
**Use**: Total value of business to all stakeholders
**Formula**: `EV = Market Cap + Total Debt - Cash`

### EV/EBITDA
**Definition**: Enterprise value divided by EBITDA
**Use**: Capital-structure-neutral valuation metric
**Formula**: `EV/EBITDA = Enterprise Value / EBITDA`

### Intrinsic Value
**Definition**: True underlying value of a business
**Use**: Compare to market price for margin of safety
**Method**: Usually via DCF analysis

### Margin of Safety
**Definition**: Discount between intrinsic value and purchase price
**Use**: Buffer against analysis errors and unforeseen events
**Target**: Minimum 25% for most investments

## Profitability Terms

### ROE (Return on Equity)
**Definition**: Net income as percentage of shareholder equity
**Use**: Measures how efficiently company uses shareholder capital
**Formula**: `ROE = Net Income / Shareholder Equity`
**Target**: > 15% for quality companies

### ROA (Return on Assets)
**Definition**: Net income as percentage of total assets
**Use**: Measures asset efficiency regardless of capital structure
**Formula**: `ROA = Net Income / Total Assets`

### ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)
**Definition**: After-tax operating income divided by invested capital
**Use**: Purest measure of capital allocation efficiency
**Formula**: `ROIC = NOPAT / (Debt + Equity - Cash)`
**Target**: ROIC > WACC = creating value

### Gross Margin
**Definition**: Revenue minus cost of goods sold, as percentage
**Use**: Measures pricing power and production efficiency
**Formula**: `Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue`

### Operating Margin
**Definition**: Operating income as percentage of revenue
**Use**: Measures operational efficiency
**Formula**: `Operating Margin = Operating Income / Revenue`

### Net Margin
**Definition**: Net income as percentage of revenue
**Use**: Measures bottom-line profitability
**Formula**: `Net Margin = Net Income / Revenue`

### EBITDA
**Definition**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization
**Use**: Cash-flow proxy, useful for comparing across capital structures
**Formula**: `EBITDA = Operating Income + D&A`

### NOPAT
**Definition**: Net Operating Profit After Tax
**Use**: Operating earnings adjusted for taxes, for ROIC calculation
**Formula**: `NOPAT = Operating Income × (1 - Tax Rate)`

---

## Cash Flow Terms

### Operating Cash Flow (OCF)
**Definition**: Cash generated from core business operations
**Use**: Measures cash generation ability
**Formula**: From cash flow statement

### Free Cash Flow (FCF)
**Definition**: Operating cash flow minus capital expenditures
**Use**: Cash available for shareholders after maintaining business
**Formula**: `FCF = Operating Cash Flow - CapEx`
**Importance**: Buffett's preferred metric for business quality

### FCF Yield
**Definition**: Free cash flow divided by market cap
**Use**: Compares cash generation to price paid
**Formula**: `FCF Yield = FCF / Market Cap`

### CapEx (Capital Expenditures)
**Definition**: Money spent on long-term assets
**Use**: Required to maintain and grow business
**Types**: Maintenance CapEx vs Growth CapEx

## Balance Sheet Terms

### Current Assets
**Definition**: Assets expected to convert to cash within one year
**Includes**: Cash, receivables, inventory, short-term investments

### Current Liabilities
**Definition**: Obligations due within one year
**Includes**: Payables, short-term debt, accrued expenses

### Current Ratio
**Definition**: Current assets divided by current liabilities
**Use**: Measures short-term liquidity
**Formula**: `Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities`
**Target**: > 1.5 for safety

### Quick Ratio (Acid Test)
**Definition**: Current assets minus inventory, divided by current liabilities
**Use**: More conservative liquidity measure
**Formula**: `Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities`

### Working Capital
**Definition**: Current assets minus current liabilities
**Use**: Operating liquidity available
**Formula**: `Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities`

### Debt-to-Equity (D/E)
**Definition**: Total debt divided by shareholder equity
**Use**: Measures financial leverage
**Formula**: `D/E = Total Debt / Shareholder Equity`
**Target**: < 0.5 conservative, < 1.0 acceptable

### Interest Coverage Ratio
**Definition**: EBIT divided by interest expense
**Use**: Ability to service debt
**Formula**: `Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense`
**Target**: > 5x for safety

### Book Value
**Definition**: Shareholder equity on balance sheet
**Use**: Liquidation proxy, basis for P/B ratio
**Formula**: `Book Value = Total Assets - Total Liabilities`

### Tangible Book Value
**Definition**: Book value minus intangible assets
**Use**: More conservative liquidation value
**Formula**: `Tangible Book Value = Book Value - Intangibles - Goodwill`

## Growth Terms

### CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
**Definition**: Smoothed annual growth rate over multiple years
**Use**: Normalizes growth for comparison
**Formula**: `CAGR = (End Value / Start Value)^(1/n) - 1`

### YoY (Year-over-Year)
**Definition**: Comparison to same period in prior year
**Use**: Removes seasonality

### QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)
**Definition**: Comparison to immediately prior quarter
**Use**: Shows short-term momentum

### Organic Growth
**Definition**: Growth from existing operations (excluding M&A)
**Use**: Measures true business expansion

### Inorganic Growth
**Definition**: Growth from acquisitions
**Use**: May indicate lack of organic opportunities

## Market Terms

### Market Capitalization
**Definition**: Total market value of company's shares
**Formula**: `Market Cap = Share Price × Shares Outstanding`

**Categories**:
- Mega Cap: > $200B
- Large Cap: $10B - $200B
- Mid Cap: $2B - $10B
- Small Cap: $300M - $2B
- Micro Cap: $50M - $300M

### Float
**Definition**: Shares available for public trading
**Formula**: `Float = Total Shares - Insider/Restricted Shares`

### Short Interest
**Definition**: Shares sold short but not yet covered
**Use**: Measures bearish sentiment
**High**: > 10-15% of float

### Short Squeeze
**Definition**: Rapid price rise forcing short sellers to cover
**Trigger**: Positive news + high short interest

### 52-Week High/Low
**Definition**: Highest and lowest prices over past year
**Use**: Range context for current price

### Beta
**Definition**: Volatility relative to market (S&P 500 = 1.0)
**Use**: Measures systematic risk
**Interpretation**: Beta > 1 = more volatile than market

## Technical Terms

### Moving Average (MA)
**Definition**: Average price over specified period
**Common**: 50-day, 200-day
**Use**: Trend identification

### Golden Cross
**Definition**: 50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA
**Signal**: Bullish

### Death Cross
**Definition**: 50-day MA crosses below 200-day MA
**Signal**: Bearish

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
**Definition**: Momentum oscillator (0-100 scale)
**Use**: Identifies overbought/oversold conditions
**Ranges**: > 70 overbought, < 30 oversold

### MACD
**Definition**: Moving Average Convergence Divergence
**Use**: Trend-following momentum indicator
**Signal**: Line crossovers

### Support
**Definition**: Price level where buying interest emerges
**Use**: Potential entry point, stop loss placement

### Resistance
**Definition**: Price level where selling pressure emerges
**Use**: Price target, breakout confirmation

### Volume
**Definition**: Number of shares traded in period
**Use**: Confirms price moves (high volume = strong move)

## Investment Terms

### Alpha
**Definition**: Returns above benchmark
**Use**: Measures manager skill
**Formula**: `Alpha = Portfolio Return - Benchmark Return`

### Beta
**Definition**: Correlation to market movements
**Use**: Measures market risk

### Sharpe Ratio
**Definition**: Risk-adjusted return
**Formula**: `Sharpe = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation`

### Drawdown
**Definition**: Peak-to-trough decline
**Use**: Measures downside risk

### Correlation
**Definition**: How assets move together (-1 to +1)
**Use**: Portfolio diversification

---

## Corporate Action Terms

### Dividend
**Definition**: Cash payment to shareholders
**Key Metrics**: Yield, Payout Ratio, Growth Rate

### Dividend Yield
**Definition**: Annual dividend divided by stock price
**Formula**: `Yield = Annual Dividend / Stock Price`

### Payout Ratio
**Definition**: Dividends as percentage of earnings
**Formula**: `Payout Ratio = Dividends / Net Income`
**Sustainable**: < 60% for most companies

### Stock Buyback (Repurchase)
**Definition**: Company buying its own shares
**Effect**: Reduces shares outstanding, increases EPS

### Stock Split
**Definition**: Increasing share count, reducing price proportionally
**Example**: 2-for-1 split doubles shares, halves price

### Reverse Split
**Definition**: Reducing share count, increasing price proportionally
**Common Reason**: To meet exchange listing requirements

### Spin-Off
**Definition**: Creating new independent company from subsidiary
**Opportunity**: Often undervalued initially

### Lockup Expiration
**Definition**: End of restricted period for insider selling (post-IPO)
**Risk**: Potential selling pressure

## Accounting Terms

### GAAP
**Definition**: Generally Accepted Accounting Principles
**Use**: Standard accounting rules in US

### Non-GAAP
**Definition**: Adjusted metrics excluding certain items
**Caution**: Can be manipulated; compare to GAAP

### Accrual Accounting
**Definition**: Recording revenue when earned, expenses when incurred
**Contrast**: Cash accounting

### Depreciation
**Definition**: Allocating cost of tangible asset over useful life
**Effect**: Non-cash expense reducing reported earnings

### Amortization
**Definition**: Allocating cost of intangible asset over useful life
**Effect**: Non-cash expense

### Goodwill
**Definition**: Premium paid over fair value in acquisition
**Risk**: Impairment if acquisition underperforms

### Stock-Based Compensation (SBC)
**Definition**: Equity awards to employees
**Effect**: Real cost often understated in non-GAAP

### Deferred Revenue
**Definition**: Payment received for not-yet-delivered goods/services
**Interpretation**: Liability that becomes revenue

### Accrued Expenses
**Definition**: Expenses incurred but not yet paid
**Example**: Wages payable, interest payable
