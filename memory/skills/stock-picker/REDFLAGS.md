# Red Flags & Warning Signs

Critical warning signs that should trigger extra scrutiny or avoidance.

---

## Financial Statement Red Flags

### Balance Sheet Warnings
| Red Flag | Why It Matters | Action |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Current ratio < 1.0 | May not meet short-term obligations | Avoid or extreme caution |
| Debt-to-equity > 2.0 | High leverage, limited flexibility | Require strong justification |
| Declining cash balance | Burning cash, may need dilution | Check burn rate vs runway |
| Goodwill > 50% of assets | Overpaid for acquisitions | Risk of writedowns |
| Off-balance sheet debt | Hidden obligations | Adjust leverage calculations |
| Inventory growing faster than sales | Demand weakness or obsolescence | Check turnover ratios |

### Income Statement Warnings
| Red Flag | Why It Matters | Action |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Revenue growth without profit growth | Pricing pressure or inefficiency | Check margins |
| One-time gains propping up earnings | Core business may be struggling | Normalize earnings |
| SG&A growing faster than revenue | Losing operational efficiency | Question management |
| Declining gross margins | Competitive pressure or cost issues | Investigate root cause |
| Revenue concentration > 20% single customer | Business risk | Assess contract stability |

### Cash Flow Warnings
| Red Flag | Why It Matters | Action |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Negative operating cash flow | Not generating cash from business | Require turnaround plan |
| Capex > Depreciation consistently | Heavy investment, lower returns | Check ROIC |
| Cash flow < Net income consistently | Low quality earnings | Adjust valuation |
| Negative FCF for 3+ years | Burning shareholder capital | Need clear path to positive |

## Accounting Red Flags

### Revenue Recognition Issues
- [ ] Channel stuffing (shipping excess inventory to distributors)
- [ ] Revenue recognition changes (more aggressive)
- [ ] High receivables relative to sales (not collecting)
- [ ] Unbilled revenue increasing rapidly
- [ ] Revenue from "round trip" transactions

### Expense Manipulation
- [ ] Capitalizing what should be expensed
- [ ] Unusual one-time charges (restructuring, impairment)
- [ ] Pension assumption changes (unrealistic returns)
- [ ] Stock-based compensation hidden or excessive
- [ ] Related party transactions

### Audit Concerns
- [ ] Auditor resignation or change
- [ ] Qualified audit opinion
- [ ] Restatements of prior period results
- [ ] Material weaknesses in internal controls
- [ ] Delayed filings (10-K, 10-Q)

### SEC & Regulatory
- [ ] SEC investigation
- [ ] Subpoenas or document requests
- [ ] Wells notice (enforcement action pending)
- [ ] Class action lawsuits
- [ ] Short seller reports with specific allegations

## Management Red Flags

### Governance Issues
- [ ] Dual-class share structure (insiders control)
- [ ] Poison pill or anti-takeover provisions
- [ ] Related party transactions with executives
- [ ] Excessive executive compensation
- [ ] Board lacks independence
- [ ] No succession planning

### Communication Issues
- [ ] Management avoids questions on earnings calls
- [ ] Guidance repeatedly missed
- [ ] Blaming external factors constantly
- [ ] Overly promotional language
- [ ] Sudden change in investor relations
- [ ] Canceling appearances at conferences

### Behavior Issues
- [ ] Insider selling at unusual times
- [ ] No insider buying during dips
- [ ] Selling shares before bad news
- [ ] Excessive stock-based compensation
- [ ] Frequent restructuring announcements
- [ ] High executive turnover

## Valuation Red Flags

### Price-Based Warnings
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| P/E > 50 | Extreme expectations priced in |
| PEG > 3.0 | Growth may not justify price |
| P/S > 20 | Revenue expectations unrealistic |
| EV/EBITDA > 25 | Very expensive on cash flow basis |
| Price > 3x intrinsic value (DCF) | Pure speculation |

### Market-Based Warnings
- [ ] Short interest > 20% of float
- [ ] Heavy retail speculation (hype on social media)
- [ ] Analyst consensus extremely bullish (> 90% buy ratings)
- [ ] Recent IPO lockup expiration approaching
- [ ] Stock heavily promoted by paid promoters

## Business Model Red Flags

### Competitive Position
- [ ] Losing market share to competitors
- [ ] Key customer defections
- [ ] Price wars in industry
- [ ] Disruptive new entrant
- [ ] Technology becoming obsolete
- [ ] Regulatory changes threatening business model

### Operational Issues
- [ ] Product quality problems or recalls
- [ ] Supply chain disruptions
- [ ] Key employee departures
- [ ] Failed product launches
- [ ] Missed production targets
- [ ] Cybersecurity breaches

### Strategic Issues
- [ ] No clear competitive advantage
- [ ] Copying competitors (no differentiation)
- [ ] Expansion into unrelated areas (diworsification)
- [ ] Frequent strategy pivots
- [ ] Overpriced acquisitions
- [ ] Dividend cut or suspension

## Industry-Specific Red Flags

### Technology
- [ ] Heavy R&D but no new products
- [ ] Dependent on single product
- [ ] Patent cliff approaching
- [ ] Tech stack becoming legacy
- [ ] Unable to attract engineering talent

### Retail
- [ ] Declining same-store sales
- [ ] Inventory buildup
- [ ] Store closures accelerating
- [ ] E-commerce transition failing
- [ ] Private label competition

### Financials
- [ ] Increasing loan loss provisions
- [ ] Declining net interest margin
- [ ] Regulatory capital concerns
- [ ] Concentration in risky assets
- [ ] Derivative exposure

### Energy
- [ ] Reserve replacement ratio < 100%
- [ ] Rising production costs
- [ ] Asset concentration in unstable regions
- [ ] Hedging book losses
- [ ] Environmental liabilities

## Behavioral Red Flags (For the Analyst)

### FOMO Signals
- [ ] Stock up significantly already
- [ ] Media coverage at peak
- [ ] "This time is different" narrative
- [ ] Buying without doing analysis
- [ ] Increasing position size to "make up" losses

### Confirmation Bias
- [ ] Only reading bullish analysis
- [ ] Dismissing bear case without consideration
- [ ] Ignoring data that contradicts thesis
- [ ] Anchoring to original purchase price

### Emotional Traps
- [ ] Averaging down without thesis change
- [ ] Refusing to sell at a loss (holding losers)
- [ ] Selling winners too early
- [ ] Overtrading
- [ ] Revenge trading after losses

## The "Walk Away" List

**Immediate Avoid if:**
1. Current ratio < 0.8 (imminent liquidity crisis)
2. Debt maturities in next 12 months > cash + credit facility
3. SEC investigation with specific fraud allegations
4. Auditor resignation without explanation
5. Management turnover in CFO, GC, or CEO roles simultaneously
6. Restatement of multiple prior periods
7. Bankruptcy or restructuring rumors with credible sources
8. Criminal charges against executives

**Extra Scrutiny Required if:**
1. Short interest > 15% of float
2. Stock down > 50% in 3 months (but thesis may still work)
3. Recent CFO departure
4. Aggressive accounting identified
5. Heavy insider selling
6. Declining industry with no turnaround plan
7. Private company with limited disclosure
8. Chinese VIE structure (regulatory risk)
